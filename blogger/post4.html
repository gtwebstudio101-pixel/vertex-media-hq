Frequently Asked Questions

How many lumens do solar path lights vs. accent spotlights actually need?

For accent and ambiance, 10–30 lumens per light is plenty — which is exactly what the VIGNUTO delivers. For actual pathway guidance, you'd want 50–100+ lumens. The VIGNUTO is strictly an accent light; pair it with higher-lumen path lights if you need to guide guests.

Do solar lights still charge in partial shade or under trees?

They do, but much slower. The VIGNUTO's panel is efficient, but direct sun is always best. Under a tree canopy, you might get 4–6 hours of run time instead of 10–14. Position them where they get at least a few hours of direct sun for best results.

What IP waterproof rating should I look for against sprinkler exposure?

IP65 is the sweet spot — it's dust-tight and protected against low-pressure water jets, which is more than enough for sprinklers and rain. The VIGNUTO's IP65 rating means it's perfectly safe for standard garden use.

Warm white vs. cool white: which attracts fewer bugs?

Warm white (2700–3000K) is generally less attractive to insects than cool white or blue-toned LEDs. The VIGNUTO uses warm-white LEDs, so it's a better choice for bug-prone evenings.
